Taste of Malaysia

Exploring how Malaysian food reflects culture, tourism and lifestyle through interactive data visualisations. This draft combines dish popularity, tourism trends, state comparisons, spice levels, meal costs and a Malaysia map to tell a clearer story about the country’s food identity.

Cuisine Popularity

1. Top Malaysian Dishes

This chart compares the popularity of iconic Malaysian dishes. The colour shows each dishs cultural origin, helping viewers understand how Malay, Chinese, Indian and Peranakan influences shape Malaysias food culture.

Tourism Trend

2. Tourism Growth

This line chart shows tourism growth over time. The sharp drop during 2020/2021 reflects the impact of travel restrictions, while the recovery afterwards shows how tourism gradually returned.

Food Economy

3. Meal Cost Breakdown

This donut chart breaks down the approximate cost of a typical Malaysian meal. It shows how protein, rice, drinks, vegetables and dessert contribute to the overall eating experience.

Flavour Profile

4. Spice Level Heatmap

This heatmap shows spice levels across different dishes and cultural origins. Darker colours represent stronger spice intensity, making it easier to compare flavour profiles across Malaysian cuisine.

State Comparison

5. Tourism by State

This bar chart compares tourist numbers across selected Malaysian states. It highlights important food and tourism destinations such as Kuala Lumpur, Penang, Johor, Sabah and Sarawak.

Geographic Story

6. Malaysia Tourism Map

This choropleth map shows tourism numbers by Malaysian state. Darker orange states represent higher tourist numbers, helping viewers identify major tourism and food-culture hotspots.

Final Insight

Conclusion

Overall, this draft shows that Malaysian food is not only about taste, but also about culture, tourism, lifestyle and economy. The next stage will add stronger annotations, improve interaction, and refine the layout into a polished final scrolling data story.
